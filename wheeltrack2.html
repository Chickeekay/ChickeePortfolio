Overview

This is a project from an international hackathon sponsored by IATA (International Air Transport Association), Microsoft, Boeing, and other major airline and travel organizations.

On this hackathon, teams were tasked to build solutions to enhance 1) the booking experience for passengers travelling with mobility aids and/or 2) the tracking of mobility aids such as wheelchairs.

Research



We initially chose to tackle the booking experience problem. Prior to the 3-day live event, teams were given the liberty to start working on their projects. So a few days before the event, I reached out to people on social media.

INSERT FACEBOOK POST
Our team also went to the SEA-TAC airport to interview the head of the official wheelchair vendor there. We also went to their control room and around the airport to observe their operations.

INSERT AIRPORT PHOTOS
INSERT interview photos
However, based on the interviews and sharings of the actual wheelchair users during the actual event, we found out that their bigger problem is the tracking of their mobility device.

We learned:
  1. Their greatest fear is learning that their wheelchairs had been damaged or got lost in transit.
  2. Most wheelchairs are highly customized depending on the need of the user.
  3. Due to their customizable nature, they are hard to repair or replace.
  4. It’s not uncommon for wheelchairs to cost as much as a car. Some are even more expensive.
  5. Reporting a damaged or lost wheelchair is a tedious process especially if there are multiple airlines & airports involved.
From the business side, we learned:
  1. Airlines and airports don’t have a centralized system for tracking wheelchairs throughout the journey.
  2. The type of battery the wheelchair has is crucial for cargo loading decision.
So despite having done pre-work for the booking problem, our team decided to pivot.

    • Branding



    Brand Name & Colors

    Because it wasn’t part of the judging criteria, we spent the least amount of time in branding. Half of our team works in Alaska Airlines so we decided to use their company color for our design.

    I came up with the brand name “WheelTrack” which is a portmanteau of “wheelchair” and “tracker”. It also has a dual meaning of “We will track” = “We’ll track your device for you.”

    Typography

    Samsung runs on Android so we used Google’s Material Design guidelines for the typography. We used Roboto for the main typeface and Lato for the headings.

    Lato was chosen due to its semi-rounded details that give a feeling of warmth, while its strong structure provides stability and seriousness.
