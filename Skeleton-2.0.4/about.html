My UX Design Journey

The roadblocks, detours and destination.

Late 80s

I was born and raised in a family of engineers and healthcare professionals. The year I was born, my country was just raising up from 14 years of martial law. So my younger years was surrounded by poverty. yet guided by happy, resourceful, and hardworking people..

UX Skills acquired:
Optimism, creativity, tech-savviness

1990s - early 2000’s

Excelled in arts and science as a kid all the way through high school. I was constantly designing, creating, and experimenting even as a hobby. I also discovered my obsession with cleaning and organizing as early as 2nd grade. I would graciously fold my entire family's laundry, and group them by purpose, size, and colors. Once a week in high school, I would volunteer to clean and organize our classroom on my own. I can very well be Marie Kondo.

UX Skills acquired:
Organization, color coordination, information architecture

2004 - 2007

Pressured by my parents, I took BS Nursing. Despite not being my first choice, it taught me a big deal about social psychology, empathy and customer service. I eventually passed both the Philippine and US licensure exams. This period coincided with the rise of digital cameras. I fell in love with photography which led me to learn Adobe Photoshop and other related software.

UX Skills earned:
Empathy, psychology, digital arts

2007 - 2008

Global recession hit. The worldwide demand for nurses declined. I took a temporary job as an HP laptop tech support for American and Canadian customers. I enjoyed it. I also landed a job with a similar role with an Australian telco company later on.

UX Skills earned:

  • + Remote communication skills with customers & team members
  • + All-around laptop hardware & software knowledge
  • + General smartphone hardware & software know-how
