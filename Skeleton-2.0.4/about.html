My UX Design Story

A journey full of roadblocks and detours.

Late 80s

Born into a family of engineers, in a country plagued with poverty but abundant with happy, ingenuious, and hardworking people.

1990s- early 2000’s

Excelled in arts as a kid. Constantly designing, creating, and tinkering stuff. I also discovered my obsession with cleaning and organizing.

2004 - 2007

Not sure of what I really want, I took BS Nursing which taught me a big deal about social psychology, empathy and customer service. I eventually passed both the Philippine and US licensure exams. This coincided with the rise of digital cameras. I fell in love with photography which led me to learn Adobe Photoshop and other related software.

2007-2008

Global recession hit. The demand of nurses worldwide declined. Took a job as laptop customer service tech support as a filler. End up really enjoying it.

2009-2012

Went back to nursing. Worked in the Middle East and was abused by my employer. Went back to the Philippines to start a new. I decided that from then on, I’m going to follow where my heart leads me.

2012-2015

I got involved in entrepreneurship. Volunteered as a photographer/designer for various projects. Worked in the life and health insurance industry. Really enjoyed the finance aspect of it. Moved to Vietnam with my then boyfriend to be his marketing arm for a language school he wanted to start. Business failed.

2016

While in Malaysia and Singapore, I applied for and got selected to represent my country at the 2016 Global Entrepreneurship Summit @ Standford University. Visa was denied but I was still able to attend the sessions remotely. Biggest lesson I got from the summit: Digital technology is extremely powerful and in the right hands, it can change people’s lives for the better.

2017

Moved to Seattle. USA to explore options in the tech industry. Attended meetups and hackathons that introduced the UX Design field to me. Eventually enrolled in Bloc’s Designer Program.

2017- now

Despite all the roadblocks and detours I had to take, I’m really excited about finally arriving and going into a field that culminates my strengths, experiences, and aspirations. The long and grueling journey had prepared me well for the work ahead.
